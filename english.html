Has the Streaming Era
Officially Transformed Into The New Cable TV?

Imagine watching your favourite show at home and, just as something is about to happen, an ad appears. Not only that, you often have to switch between services to find your shows. Streaming services are now showing similarities with cable TV, which annoys many people.

The Problem

Too Crowded & Separated

There is no doubt that streaming services have become way too crowded and separated. Instead of having just one or two main services to watch our favourite shows, the streaming industry is separated into too many different services.

Exclusive Content

This is mainly because some streaming services have exclusive content that they use to attract the customer's attention, forcing viewers to pay multiple subscriptions just to get the same amount of shows they used to get in one place.

The Unified Past 📦

One Central Place

  • ✓ We used to only need 1 streaming service like Netflix (at maximum 2, like Prime Video).
  • ✓ All your movies and series were kept inside a simple, practical, and fun environment.
The Frustrating Present 🧩

Fragmented Platforms

  • ✕ We are now forced to pay for multiple different platforms like Hulu, Disney+, Apple TV and more.
  • ✕ The mess of managing all of these subscriptions proves streaming has become a frustrating chore.
Incredibly Expensive

The Cost of Stacking Subscriptions

There is no doubt that subscribing to multiple services at the same time has become incredibly expensive. This rising cumulative cost completely defeats the original purpose of cheap, ad-free streaming.

Tactics & Quality

Worse Plans, Growing Prices

Streaming services keep raising their prices for monthly plans, but instead of giving us more benefits, they are doing the opposite. They are forcing us into cheaper plans flooded with ads or making us pay a premium to get rid of them.

Ad-Supported Influx

Almost every major streaming platform (like Netflix, Disney+ and Prime Video) has recently created ad-supported plans or raised subscription prices.

Premium Ad-Free Bundle Price Evolution +110% Rise Since 2018
Autonomy Fallacy

The "Freedom" Illusion

Contrastingly, a lot of people believe that streaming is still better than cable because it gives us the freedom and accessibility to watch whatever we want, whenever we want, without being stuck to a fixed schedule. However, this freedom is mostly inexistent.

🔄

Platform Migration

If your favorite show leaves Netflix and moves to another platform, you are basically forced to buy another subscription just to keep watching it.

🔒

Password Lockdowns

Sharing features and access parameters are highly restricted, converting what was a flexible option into a highly restrictive ecosystem.

📢

Not Free At All

What looks like a convenient choice ends up being a stressful task of managing different passwords and bills.

Final Summary
"We didn't actually escape cable...
we just let it change its name."
